## 1ï¸âƒ£ DEEP DIVE: The "Last Mile" Distribution Challenge

Why does agritech fail where fintech succeeded? Because you can't UPI your way into a farmer's trust.

**The Brutal Math of Rural Distribution:**
- Average Indian village: 500-2000 people
- Nearest mandi (market): 15-30 km away
- Smartphone penetration: 60-70% but only 30% use data regularly
- Average farmer income: â‚¹10,000/month (can't afford subscription SaaS)

**What Works (DeHaat model):**
- "Micro-entrepreneurs" in each village (local shopkeeper becomes DeHaat agent)
- Agent earns commission on every input sold, loan facilitated, crop sold
- Farmer trusts the local person, not the app
- Tech is invisibleâ€”farmer calls/WhatsApps the agent, agent uses the app

**What Fails:**
- Pure digital plays expecting farmers to download apps
- B2C models without village-level presence
- Subscription pricing (farmers prefer pay-per-transaction)

**Startup Insight:** Win the village shopkeeper, and you win the village. The best agritech companies are essentially franchise models with tech underneath.
